My name is Bing Wang.

I am a person brimming with curiosity about the world, with a passion for delving into the unknown. My interests are broad and varied, encompassing everything from programming to scientific research, and from physical body to mathematics—all integral parts of my life.

Mathematics is not just the discipline I hold in the highest regard; it is also a crucial component of my worldview. I have a tattoo that climbers can instantly recognize as a figure-eight knot, but to me, it represents more than its topological structure. It is a symbol of mathematical beauty, with a unique and singular way of drawing it under certain constraints, much like a circle. If I could fulfill one wish before I die, it would be to grasp the essence of the Riemann Hypothesis.

Programming, to me, is a precise and unambiguous dialogue, with immediate feedback that allows me to see the realization of my ideas or their flaws instantly. The core aspects of programming, such as algorithms, data structures, and design patterns, I regard as extensions of mathematics, constituting the beauty of coding.

I once devoted myself to scientific research and underwent rigorous training, but ultimately, I chose to quit. In modern research, most of the low-hanging fruit has been picked, and professional researchers must spend their lives in pursuit of knowledge in a tiny specific field. Research as a profession and as a hobby are not the same, and today, I prefer to be an enthusiast, exploring new discoveries across various disciplines through open databases like arXiv to satisfy my curiosity.

My career has been diverse: transitioning from academia to programming, I've moved from Python scripting to backend and frontend development, delved into image algorithms, ventured into natural language processing, worked with Java design patterns, software architecture, and even written a regex compiler. Although the rise of GPT4 led to the dissolution of my project team, GPT4 itself has become a valuable assistant to me.

Beyond my professional path, I also practice Aikido, Yoga, Rock Climbing, and Contact Improvisation. After leaving my last job in 2022, my spouse and I took over the ShangHai Contact Improvisation community. Together, we manage the community, serve the neighborhood, teach classes, host workshops, and have recently started planning an arts festival. These activities are not just about physical body exploration; they are about valuing human connections, engaging in sincere dialogue, and truly seeing each other.

Recently, I have turned my attention to global warming. Learning about carbon emissions and extreme weather has made me anxious about humanity's future, but also deeply curious. If the future holds a "bad ending," I hope to contribute in some way to averting it. I want to understand how technology can help us face potential future disasters.

Every time I enter a new field, I aim to gain a deep understanding, whether it's expanding from programming to product management, testing, project management, or from performing arts to stage direction, lighting, and theater management. I yearn to experience the world and understand how it operates. Sometimes I wonder why an employer would want someone so undisciplined in their career path. But then I remember that life is mine to live. In this era, I'm not worried about survival; there's always a way to live for one's passions and interests. I believe there will always be appreciation for someone as diverse and curious as I am.

I love this world, whether it's through technology, art, or environmental protection, and I hope my existence can make it a slightly better place. I hope you, too, share this love for our world.
